2.6.5.      Sourcing methods

Visual – TBD with graphic designer

Sourcing methods

-          Competitive

-          Non-competitive

The Government Contract Regulations (GCRs) require that the competitive solicitation of bids be done before any contract is entered into. However, if one or more of the GCRs exceptions apply, contracts may be entered into without soliciting bids.

Good practice:

  • Use a competitive process, even when the procurement is restricted to land claim beneficiary businesses, provided it meets the requirements.
  • If the beneficiary business can not meet the requirement, re-solicit, and leverage the PSAB to set the requirement aside for Indigenous businesses from across Canada and include IPC measures such as subcontracting to beneficiary businesses or;
  • Solicit bids from all businesses across Canada and include IPC measures such as subcontracting to beneficiary businesses.

Impact of modern treaty/CLCA obligations on the sourcing method selection.

As always, keep applicable modern treaty/CLCA obligations in mind as you select the appropriate sourcing method.

Question 1

Answer options

Feedback

A department has a requirement for a mini excavator and utility vehicle in Ivvavik National Park, NWT. The requirement is:

·         valued at $80,000 and

·         subject to a right of first refusal obligation.

After searching the  Inuvialuit Business List (IBL), you determine that five (5) Inuvialuit businesses have the capacity to supply you with the requirement.

For each of the sourcing methods described, identify if they can be applied to this procurement, then select Submit.

a) Use a competitive process to solicit bids from all five (5) businesses listed in the IBL. 

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : Yes, it can be applied.

You must invite all five Indigenous firms on the Inuvialuit Business List provide a bid. In other words, limit bids to Inuvialuit businesses only.

b) Use a competitive process to solicit bids from Inuvialuit and non-Indigenous businesses

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : No, it can not be applied.

Given that there are Indigenous businesses from the Inuvialuit Business List that may be able to do the work, and because they have a right of first refusal, you must limit bids amongst them.

c) Use a non-competitive process and grant a sole-source contract to one of the qualified Indigenous business from the IBL.

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : No, it can not be applied.

As with any procurement, a non-competitive sourcing strategy must be in accordance with the GCRs.

Because the requirement is above the GCR threshold, is subject to a right of first refusal obligation and there are Indigenous firms on the IBL that have the capacity to supply you with the requirement, you must invite all five (5) Inuvialuit businesses to provide a bid. In other words, limit bids to Inuvialuit businesses only.

Question 2

Answer options

Feedback

Your department requires event planning services for a conference being held in Delta, BC.  This is subject to the Tsawwassen First Nation Final Agreement.

The requirement is:

·         valued at $80,000 and

·         not subject to a right of first refusal obligation.

The Tsawwassen Indigenous business directory indicates that one (1) Indigenous-owned business meets the requirements. 

For each of the sourcing methods described, identify if they can be applied to this procurement, then select Submit.

a) Use a competitive process to solicit bids and issue an open tender to allow all Canadian suppliers and Indigenous suppliers to bid.

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : Yes, it can be applied.

The fact that a request is subject to a modern treaty/CLCA and there is an Indigenous firm that can meet the requirements  is not enough grounds for a sole-source contract. Because the requirement is not subject to a right of first refusal obligation, you may use an open competitive process. The Government Contracts Regulations (GCRs) still apply, meaning that it is the department’s responsibility to ensure that a competitive process is followed unless a GCR exception to competition applies.

b) Use a non-competitive process and grant a sole-source contract to the Indigenous business provided it meets requirements.

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : No, it can not be applied.

The fact that a contract is subject to a modern treaty/CLCA and is an Indigenous business  is not enough grounds for a sole-source contract. Because the requirement is not subject to a right of first refusal obligation, a competitive process must be followed to ensure that all businesses are given the opportunity to bid unless a GCR exception to competition applies.

c) Use a competitive process and set aside the requirement under PSAB.

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : Yes, it can be applied.

In the absence of right of first refusal in a modern treaty/CLCA area, business owners with assistance from contracting authorities can set aside the procurement under PSAB while ensuring the provisions of applicable modern treaties/CLCAs or National Park Co-management Agreement are complied with.

d) Use a non-competitive process and sole source to a business registered under PSAB.

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : No, it can not be applied.

The fact that a requirement is not subject to a right of first refusal obligation, and there is a PSAB firm that can meet the requirements is not enough grounds for a sole-source contract. To ensure an open, fair and transparent procurement process,  the business owner and contracting authority must consult the Indigenous Business Directory and determine if there are other PSAB firms that can do the work.

Feedback

The department is hosting a smaller workshop, this time in Yellowknife, NWT and requires AV Services.

The requirement is:

·         valued at $9,000

·         not subject to a right of first refusal obligation.

The Tlicho business directory indicates that one (1) Tlicho-owned business from the Tlicho Land Claims and Self Government Agreement settlement area has the capacity to supply the requirement.

For each of the sourcing methods described, identify if they can be applied to this procurement, then select Submit.

a) Use a competitive process to solicit bids from Indigenous businesses from the Tlicho settlement area.

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : No, it can not be applied.

Given that the requirement is not subject to a right of first refusal obligation, you cannot use a competitive process to solicit bids from Tlicho businesses.

b) Use a non-competitive (sole source) process to award a sole-source contract to the Tlicho-owned business.

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : Yes, it can be applied.

As with any procurement, a non-competitive sourcing strategy must be in accordance with the GCRs (in this case, under the dollar threshold). A sole source contract may be awarded to the Tlicho Land Claims and Self Government Agreement business provided it meets the requirements.

c) Use a competitive process and set aside the requirement under PSAB.

q  Yes, it can be applied.

q  No, it can not be applied.

Your answer is correct/incorrect. 

The answer is : Yes, it can be applied.

Because the requirement is not subject right of first refusal, the requirement could be completed under PSAB, provided the obligations of the Tlicho Land Claims and Self Government Agreement are met.
