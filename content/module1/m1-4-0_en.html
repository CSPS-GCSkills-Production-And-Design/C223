2.5.1.      What you need to know

Defining the requirement is the cornerstone of a successful procurement process. Business owners are responsible for leading this step, but it is equally important that you know what it entails.

The answer is : Provide guidance and advice

Being the trusted advisor.

Your level of involvement during this step will depend on the business owner’s level of experience with procurement and on the complexity of the requirement. The business owner might not know about modern treaties/CLCAs and may rely on your knowledge to ensure the applicable obligations are met.

Because modern treaty/CLCA obligations affect many phases of the procurement process, it is also to your advantage to be informed and involved early so you can plan and adapt your procurement activities accordingly.

There are two important activities to complete to ensure the requirement and resulting procurement activities address modern treaty/CLCA obligations:

  1. Determine if the procurement is subject to any modern treaty/CLCA;
  2. Identify and understand applicable modern treaty procurement obligations.
