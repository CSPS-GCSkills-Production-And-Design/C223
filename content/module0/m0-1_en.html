1.2.        A note on terminology

 

Indigenous versus Aboriginal

The terms “Indigenous Peoples” and “Aboriginal peoples” refer to the same group. However, they are not interchangeable within the legal context.

  • The term “Aboriginal” has specific meaning and legal accuracy in the context of section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982, which states that Aboriginal peoples “include the Indian, Inuit and Métis peoples of Canada.”
  • Unlike the term “Aboriginal peoples”, “Indigenous Peoples” has not been legally defined in statute or by the courts in Canada. However, the Government of Canada generally uses the term “Indigenous Peoples” to refer to the First Nations, Inuit and Métis of Canada.

What terminology will be used in this course?

To ensure that the terminology is used consistently and aligns with that of the federal government, the term “Indigenous” will be used throughout the course. “Aboriginal” will be maintained if it is part of a strategy, policy or agreement title.
