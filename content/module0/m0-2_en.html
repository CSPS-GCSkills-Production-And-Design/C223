Learning objectives

If any of these statements sound familiar, then this course will be beneficial for you. It delves into two distinct yet interrelated areas that have an impact on federal government contracting activities: the Procurement Strategy for Aboriginal Business (PSAB) and Modern Treaties, also known as Comprehensive Land Claims Agreements (CLCAs).

At the end of this course, you will be able to:

  • determine the applicability of Modern Treaties, also known as Comprehensive Land Claims Agreements (CLCAs), and key mechanisms of the Procurement Strategy for Aboriginal Business (PSAB)
  • describe the common procurement obligations that may apply when a procurement is subject to a Modern Treaty/CLCA
  • explain how the applicability of Modern Treaty/CLCA obligations and the use of PSAB mechanisms might affect certain steps in the procurement process

Course completion

The course has been divided into two modules and there are two final exams.  

  • You must obtain a passing grade of 70% for each module to successfully complete the course.
  • Each exam should take you around 10 minutes.
  • You may access the course materials during the test and you may redo the test as many times as you need to obtain a passing grade.
